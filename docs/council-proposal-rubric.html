Proposal scoring rubric

Author

Jason Wallin, OEDA-GIS Manager

Background

Proposals for OEDA projects will be written by the OEDA researchers, generally in collaboration with researchers, data experts, or subject matter experts from partner agencies.

Proposals focus on

  • the current circumstances that partner agencies face: successes, problems, and opportunities
  • the future state that agencies would like to see, if those matters are addressed
  • the research questions that, when answered, can help achieve the future state
  • the research solutions that, when complete, will provide answers to the research questions

Screening proposals

OEDA staff will screen proposals ensuring that they meet a few must-have dimensions required of all OEDA proposals.

Screening item 1: Integrated

  • Text: The proposal integrates data from more than one agency.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are cross-agency and collaborative
Score Desc
TRUE The proposed work integrates data from more than one state agency.
FALSE The proposed work does not integrate data from more than one state agency.

Screening item 2: Redisclosure

  • Text: The proposal precludes redisclosure to third parties.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are ethical
Score Desc
TRUE The proposal explicitly precludes the redisclosure of agency data to anyone other than the contributing agency and OEDA.
FALSE The proposal does not contain language precluding redisclosure.

Screening item 3: HumanSubjects

  • Text: The proposal addresses human subjects protections.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are ethical
Score Desc
TRUE The proposal provides reasoning that the project does not meet the federal definition of research, so IRB review is not required.
TRUE The proposal provides reasoning that the project is research, but does not include human subjects, so IRB review is not required.
TRUE The proposed work is human subjects research and meets one or more of the requirements for review by the Public Health Institutional Review Board. The proposal indicates that chartered work will need review from that body.
TRUE The proposed work is human subjects research, does not meet any of the requirements for review by the PH-IRB, but includes an attestation from someone other than the investigators that the research is exempt from IRB review.
TRUE The proposed work is human subjects research and is not exempt. The proposal indicates that chartered work will need review from an IRB.
FALSE None of the above are true; the proposed work does not adequately address human subjects protections.

Scoring proposals

Members of the OEDA Advisory Council will score proposals on criteria that relate to the relevance, impact, and urgency of the proposed work.

Scored item 1: Importance

  • Text: The current state is important to address.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are relevant and impactful
Score Desc
1 The issue is relevant to a small work unit within a single agency and affects tens of clients.
2 The issue is relevant to several employees across one or two agencies and affects hundreds of clients.
3 The issue is relevant to employees across several agencies, relates to a state priority, and affects several hundreds of clients.
4 The issue is widespread across multiple agencies, relates to one or more key state priorities, and affects thousands of clients.
5 The issue is foundational and cross-agency, with substantial relationships to effective service delivery, and affects tens of thousands of clients.

Scored item 2: Urgency

  • Text: The current state is urgent to address.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are relevant and impactful
Score Desc
1 There is no immediate need to address this issue. It can be revisited at a later date with little to no consequence.
2 The issue should be addressed within the next two years, but there are no immediate deadlines or critical events tied to it.
3 The issue needs to be addressed within the year to avoid a negative impact on services or a key initiative.
4 The issue needs to be addressed soon to support an upcoming policy change, a legislative mandate, or a critical program launch.
5 The issue requires immediate attention to avoid a substantial negative outcome, such as a service disruption, public harm, or failure to meet a legal deadline.

Scored item 3: Impact

  • Text: The future state is an improvement to agency operations and client services.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are relevant and impactful
Score Desc
1 The proposed future state offers minimal improvements over the status quo, with little potential to change how agencies operate or serve clients.
2 The future state would represent localized, loosely described improvements within one or two state agencies.
3 The future state would be a clear, measureable improvement in specific processes or services across multiple agencies.
4 The future state would be a significant change in how agencies coordinate or make decisions, a new level of efficiency, service quality, or service equity.
5 The future state is a new paradigm for data-informed decision making across multiple agencies, fundamentally changing how government operates and serves its citizens.

Scored item 4: Questions

  • Text: The research questions are meaningful and useful.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are relevant and impactful
Score Desc
1 The research topics or questions are vague, unrelated to the core problems or opportunities of the current state, or would not meaningfully contribute to achieving the desired future state.
2 The research topics or questions are too broad, too narrow, or would only provide a partial answer. Significant uncertainty would remain.
3 The questions are clear and directly address the gap between current and future states. Answering the questions would provide the necessary insight to move from the current to future state with reasonable confidence.
4 The questions are insightful and precise, pinpointing the critical information needed to make informed decisions. Answering the questions is likely not to just fill the gap, but reveal new opportunities.
5 The questions are incisive, originally, and essential for project success. Answering these questions would eliminate major uncertainties and provide a high degree of confidence that the desired future state can be achieved.

Scored item 5: Equity

  • Text: The proposal future state is an improvement to agency operations and client services.
  • OEDA value: OEDA projects are ethical
Score Desc
1 The proposal does not adequately acknowledge or consider ethical or equity implications; or there are reasons to believe the work will cause harm or perpetuate existing biases.
2 The proposal mentions ethics or equity, but superficially, promising to meet minimum compliance standards, but without specific details or reasoning, and disconnected from the core project.
3 The proposal includes specific plans related to ethics and equity, like data disaggregation, or including thoughts from those with lived experiences. But improving service equity is not a primary focus of the work.
4 The proposal demonstrates a thoughtful understanding of the ethical and equity landscapeclearly identifying regions, communities, populations relevant to the research questions and providing strong rationale for their inclusion. A project aim is improving service equity, fairness, and equality of outcomes.
5 The proposal includes meaningful engagement with clients in different parts of the state, with a variety of lived experiences, and part of different racial, ethnic, cultural, linguistic, and other communities. Specific metrics for measuring equity are incorporated into the proposed work, and improving those measures is key to the work.

IRB

The proposal provides reasoning that the project does not meet the federal definition of research, so IRB review is not required

Research means a systematic investigation, including research development, testing, and evaluation, designed to develop or contribute to generalizable knowledge [45 CFR 46.102(l)]

Many common state agency analytics projects involve development and evaluation, but are nonetheless not research. This is generally because they are not designed to develop or contribute to generalizable knowledge. That is, they are not seeking findings that can be applied to populations or situations beyond those studied.

Typical systematic uses of data that are not research include:

  • Internal program evaluation and quality improvement efforts
  • Operational activities
  • Informational interviews

The proposal provides reasoning that the project is research, but does not include human subjects, so IRB review is not required

Human subject means a living individual about whom an investigator (whether professional or student) conducting research: (i) Obtains information or biospecimens through intervention or interaction with the individual, and uses, studies, or analyzes the information or biospecimens (ii) Obtains, uses, studies, analyzes, or generates identifiable private information on identifiable biospecimens 45 CFR 46.102(e)

Often, work with administrative data is neither the result of intervention nor interaction.

Intervention includes both physical procedures by which information or biospecimens are gathered (e.g., venipuncture) and manipulations of the subject or the subjects environment that are performed for research purposes. 45 CFR 46.102(e)(2)

Generally, researchers for state agencies are not able to create randomized control trials where Oregonians experiences with their government are manipulated to develop strong internal validity for generalizable claims.

Interaction includes communication or interpersonal contact between investigator and subject 45 CFR 46.102(e)(3)

Researchers for state agencies are often working with private information.

Private information includes information about behavior that occurs in a context in which an individual can reasonably expect that no observation or recording is taking place, and information that has been provided for specific purposes by an individual and that the individual can reasonably expect will not be made public (e.g., a medical record). 45 CFR 46.102(e)(4)

But, researchers can often answer meaningful agency questions without using identifiable private information.

Identifiable private information is private information for which the identity of the subject is or may readily be ascertained by the investigator or associated with the information. 45 CFR 46.102(e)(5)

Analyses using deidentified data, which cannot be reasonably reidentified by the investigator, are generally not considered human subjects, as individual humans can no long be connected to the attributes being studied.

Creating analytical datasets which follow Safe Harbor Regulations is one way to ensure that the analysis does not involve human subjects.

Any study that meets any of the criteria below must seek review by the Public Health Institutional Review Board.

Public Health Institutional Review Board (p. 3)

For context, the PH and MCHD Institutional Review Board requires expedited review by the Board to determine whether a protocol is exempt.

Exempt research includes (among other exemptions):

  • Research in educational settings involving normal educational practices 45 CFR 46.104(d)(1)
  • Research whose interactions are only educational tests or observation of public behavior 45 CFR 46.104(d)(2)
  • Research whose interventions are benign behavioral interventions 45 CFR 46.104(d)(3)
  • Secondary research for which consent is not required 45 CFR 46.104(d)(4), including specifically the following

The research involves only information collection and analysis involving the investigators use of identifiable health information when that use is regulated under 45 CFR parts 160 and 164, subparts A and E, for the purposes of health care operations or research as those terms are defined at 45 CFR 164.501 or for public health activities and purposes as described under 45 CFR 164.512(b);
